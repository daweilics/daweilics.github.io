THE WORKSHOP SCOPE

Recent rapid advances in Mobile Cellular Networks, Cloud Computing, and Big Data have introduced Fog/Edge Computing as an interesting topic to the research community. Since its first appearance, the Fog/Edge Computing has received much attention from practitioners, scholars, and the general public. Considering that we are in the era of big data, many applications, such as the Internet of Things, Mobile Social Networks, and Smart City, keep generating data and interacting with the remote data centers all the time, which creates unique requirements and new challenges on the computation, storage, and communication. Thanks to the idea of the Fog/Edge Computing, tasks can be mitigated from the heavy-weighted data center to local devices, which not only reduces the workload of the data center but also accelerates the average serving speed. Many new techniques have been developed and are adopted in the Fog/Edge Computing. Due to the benefits of using Fog/Edge Computing, many emergent applications are now available for individuals, business enterprises, and governments. To this end, this workshop aims to create a platform for scholars, industry professionals, and government officials to discuss and share new research ideas and technologies in the Edge/Fog Computing and related applications.





TOPICS OF INTEREST

This workshop solicits original, unpublished contributions in all aspects of fog/edge computing, from a fog device to cyber-physical systems to Internet of Things, from theory to systems and applications. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Mobile Cloud Computing Systems
  • Data centers for Fog/Edge Computing
  • Infrastructures for Fog/Edge Computing
  • Security Issues in Fog/Edge Computing
  • Privacy Issues in Fog/Edge Computing
  • Cloudlet based computing
  • Cloud computing
  • Resource allocation, scheduling, and management
  • Resources monitoring and reporting mechanism
  • Middleware for Fog/Edge infrastructures
  • Applications of Fog/Edge Computing
  • Modelling of Fog/Edge Computing
  • Fog/Edge storage and management
  • Deployment strategies of Fog/Edge infrastructures
  • Quality of Service in Fog/Edge Computing
  • Edge-to-Fog-to-Cloud
  • Latency-critical Fog/Edge applications
  • Locality-critical Fog/Edge applications
  • Communications in Fog/Edge Computing Systems
  • Big Data in Fog/Edge Computing
  • Artificial Intelligence in Fog/Edge Computing
  • Blockchain in Fog/Edge Computing
  • Internet of Things in Fog/Edge Computing
  • Software Defined Networks in Fog/Edge Computing

REVIEW AND PUBLICATION OF MANUSCRIPTS

Submitted papers will be reviewed by the workshop Program Committee and judged on originality, technical correctness, relevance, and quality of presentation. An accepted paper must be presented at the IEEE MASS 2021 venue by one of the authors registered at the full registration rate. Each workshop registration covers up to two workshop papers by an author. Accepted and presented papers will be published in the MASS proceedings and submitted to IEEE Xplore as well as other Abstracting and Indexing (A&I) databases. IEEE reserves the right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference, including IEEE Xplore® Digital Library if the paper is not presented by the author at the conference.
